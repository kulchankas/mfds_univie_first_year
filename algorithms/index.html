⭐ Exam-priority proofs

The proofs the professor flagged as exam-relevant. Master these end-to-end.

General proof techniques
  • Loop invariants — prove correctness by initialization (holds before the loop), maintenance (preserved by each iteration), and termination (gives the desired result).
  • Master Theorem — solving divide-and-conquer recurrences $T(n)=a\,T(n/b)+f(n)$ by comparing $f(n)$ with $n^{\log_b a}$.
  • Akra–Bazzi theorem — the general recurrence method for unequal / non-uniform subproblem sizes, where the Master Theorem doesn't apply.
